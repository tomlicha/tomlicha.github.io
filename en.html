Projects

Virtual Reality / Surgeon simulator

Internship University of Würzburg - Germany • February 2018 - May 2018

Creation of a Operating Room simulator to train surgeons for the manipulation of a C-ARM. Research and development internship in a laboratory at the University of Würzburg (Bayern, Germany).
“Does this robot arm fit into the environment of the operating room?” “What if the surgeon has to move the OR light?” A lot of questions need to be answered and possible solutions need to be adapted until they work.
Finally product trainers want to understand how the newest products work in order to provide training material to the customers. Currently they often use intermediate development states as well as the final product as a base. This means that preparation is possible only very late in the development process. How can we make it possible to have a realistic experience of our future products? So realistic that we can create a feeling for quality and interaction between several integrated components?
The goal of my work basically is to answer the following questions with the support of virtual reality:
- How can human beings experience interactive systems and their dimensions in a virtual environment so they can evaluate its quality?
- Is it possible to mix hardware user interfaces with virtual reality in a way that users can have a real life experience (e.g. haptic feedback) in a virtual space?
- Which infrastructure is needed to enable designers to easily get their 3D designs into a virtual space so that they can experience the implications of their design decisions?

IoT

Internship Triskalia (29 Finistère) • June 2017 - August 2017

Internship of 12 weeks at Triskalia (29). Development of a web application using PL/SQL, HTML, JavaScript, Python.
The aim of this application is to set up connected objects in farms in order to have real-time monitoring breeding variables such as humidity, CO2 level, temperature, Ammonia content in the air ... I have also set up web alert, SMS to optimize breeding conditions and to be able to detect and prvent diseases. At the customer's request, I have also developed a web client to visualize the data as graphs showing the evolution of the different levels. The identification is made by Google OAuth. This website has a section dedicated to Triskalia technicians and another dedicated to members whose operations are monitored by IoT. This separation is intended to provide the farmeronly the data related to their farms and to the technician, a list of farms he is monitoring so he can select the one he wants to see the data.
